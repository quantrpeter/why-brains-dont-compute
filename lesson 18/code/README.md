# Lesson 18: Stereopsis

An empirical alternative to computational theories of binocular depth perception: binocular circuitry can arise from evolved neural networks using anatomical (rather than image) correspondence, and depth perception reflects the frequency of occurrence of binocular differences in experience.

## Key Concepts

### Image vs Anatomical Correspondence
The standard theory of stereopsis holds that the brain matches corresponding points in the two retinal images to compute disparity and thus depth. An alternative proposes anatomical correspondence—matching based on the physical layout of the retinas and their projections—rather than image content. This reframes the problem: the circuitry may reflect evolved solutions to recurring binocular input patterns, not a geometric computation of 3D structure.

### Binocular Circuitry from Evolved Neural Networks
When artificial neural networks are evolved to process binocular input under ecological constraints, they develop circuitry that resembles biological binocular neurons. Ocular dominance—where neurons are dominated by input from one eye—emerges naturally, as does segregation into cortical stripes (ocular dominance columns). This suggests that the architecture of binocular processing may be an empirical outcome of evolution, not a hand-designed algorithm for disparity computation.

### Empirical Depth Perception
The frequency of occurrence of binocular differences (disparities) in natural scenes predicts perceived depth. Just as lightness, color, and motion reflect empirical ranking, stereoscopic depth reflects accumulated experience with the statistical structure of binocular input. The horopter—the locus of points that project to corresponding retinal locations—and depth scaling can be understood in this framework without assuming that the brain "computes" 3D geometry.

## Discussion Questions

1. How does the anatomical correspondence alternative differ from Marr and Poggio's computational theory of stereo vision?
2. Why might evolved neural networks develop ocular dominance stripes without explicit programming for them?
3. In what sense is stereoscopic depth perception "empirical" rather than computational?
