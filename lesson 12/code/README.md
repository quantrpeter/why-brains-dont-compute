# Lesson 12: Empirical Ranking

Stimuli act as recurrent patterns; the frequency of their occurrence determines perceptual quality through empirical ranking. The luminance-lightness relationship and complex contrast effects are predicted by this framework, with a feedback loop linking perception to survival.

## Key Concepts

### Stimuli as Recurrent Patterns
Images activate retinal receptors in unique patterns, but simple recurring patterns are key. The brain does not process each image as novel; it responds to patterns that have occurred before. The frequency with which a pattern has been associated with particular behavioral outcomes shapes how it is perceived. Recurrence is the basis of empirical ranking.

### Luminance and Lightness: Frequency Determines Perception
Nearby surfaces in natural scenes share reflectance and illumination statistics. The frequency of luminance co-occurrences—how often a given luminance pattern appears in a given context—determines perceived lightness. Percentile rank of luminance co-occurrences predicts contrast effects: a gray patch ranks differently depending on whether it appears against light or dark surrounds, matching psychophysical data.

### The Feedback Loop to Survival
Empirical rank influences perception; perception guides behavior; behavior affects survival; survival shapes the gene pool and future experience. This feedback loop ensures that perceptual rankings are tuned to what has worked in the past. The framework is wholly empirical—no computation of physical reality, only statistical association refined by consequences.

## Discussion Questions

2. Why is the feedback loop to survival essential for the empirical framework?
3. What predictions does empirical ranking make that could distinguish it from other theories of perception?
